My Box: The Trap of Immediate Pleasure

My box is the pull toward instant gratification. When I'm in a structured environment like rugby or work, I'm as motivated and hardworking as anyone. But the moment I'm left to my own devices, I struggle.

Default Habits

This box exists because my brain is wired for short-term rewards, and I've allowed these habits to become my default. I want to elongate my attention span and be comfortable in boredom—to grow to enjoy the peace instead of needing something to fill it.

Expanding My Box

Meeting Grant Keenan reinforced that high performance starts with structure and visualization. Keenan was the backs and skills coach for the New Zealand Black Ferns when they won the 2017 Women's Rugby World Cup and became the first women's team ever named World Rugby Team of the Year. He has since served as High Performance Manager for the Hino Red Dolphins in Japan Rugby League One and currently serves as a technical advisor to the U.S. national rugby team and director of Crusaders International Academy. When he visited Miami University this year to coach our rugby team for a week, his approach to weekly planning showed me how to bridge the gap between ambitious vision and daily execution.

My Commitment: SMART Goals for the Next 6 Weeks

1

The Digital Detox Tax

Any minute spent on unproductive phone use (YouTube, Clash Royale, etc.) will cost me 1 push-up. I will use my phone's screen time tracker to log this and complete all owed push-ups at the end of each day before going to sleep.

2

Launching My CFA Journey

I will start my CFA Level 1 prep by first contacting two CFA charterholders from my Johnson internship and consulting with my dad by the end of this week for their study advice. Based on their input, I will then study for 2 total hours each week, split across at least two sessions.

3

The Sunday System

Every Sunday evening, I will block out 30 minutes to plan my week. This includes blocking time in my Google Calendar for my 2 hours of CFA studying and reviewing both my push-up penalty performance and how well I followed my plan from the previous week.

Moving Forward

My HBDI shows green as my lowest quadrant—the structured, process driven thinking that turns goals into reality. This plan directly challenges me to strengthen that weakness. Through these systems, I'm not just fighting distraction; I'm training myself to find satisfaction in discipline, to embrace the discomfort of delayed gratification, and to build the mental endurance my future demands.

This is how I transform intention into action. 🚀
